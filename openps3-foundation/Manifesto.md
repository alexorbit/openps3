# OpenPS3 Manifesto

## We Believe Hardware Should Outlive Corporate Decisions

The PlayStation 3 is one of the most significant computing platforms ever created.

It introduced the Cell Broadband Engine, pushed distributed computing research, enabled groundbreaking games, and inspired an entire generation of developers, researchers, artists, and enthusiasts.

Millions of fully functional consoles still exist today.

Yet their future is increasingly determined by the availability of proprietary online services, closed firmware, and infrastructure that will inevitably disappear.

When a device remains functional but loses its software ecosystem, society loses more than a product.

It loses knowledge.

It loses history.

It loses a platform for creation.

OpenPS3 exists to ensure that does not happen.

---

# Our Mission

OpenPS3 is an open-source effort dedicated to preserving, documenting, and extending the PlayStation 3 as an independent computing platform.

Our goal is not to replace its history.

Our goal is to secure its future.

We seek to transform the PS3 from a discontinued consumer product into a permanent open computing platform that anyone can study, develop for, and improve.

---

# Why

Every discontinued platform follows the same path.

Online services disappear.

Stores close.

Certificates expire.

Downloads vanish.

Documentation is lost.

Knowledge becomes fragmented.

Eventually, hardware that still works becomes increasingly difficult to use.

This should not be the destiny of the PlayStation 3.

A computer does not stop being useful because its manufacturer stops supporting it.

---

# What OpenPS3 Is

OpenPS3 is an ecosystem.

It includes:

* Open documentation
* Open development tools
* Open SDK
* Open operating environment
* Open package ecosystem
* Open developer APIs
* Open hardware research
* Community-maintained software

OpenPS3 belongs to everyone.

No single company owns its future.

---

# What OpenPS3 Is Not

OpenPS3 is not built around piracy.

It is not intended to facilitate copyright infringement.

It is not intended to replace legitimate ownership of software.

Instead, OpenPS3 focuses on:

* homebrew development
* preservation
* education
* research
* experimentation
* interoperability
* accessibility
* long-term sustainability

---

# Core Principles

## Freedom

Users should control the hardware they own.

A computer should continue to function regardless of the commercial status of its manufacturer.

---

## Preservation

Every generation deserves access to the technological achievements of previous generations.

Digital preservation is cultural preservation.

---

## Transparency

Every component developed by OpenPS3 should be documented.

Knowledge hidden inside proprietary implementations eventually disappears.

Knowledge shared openly survives.

---

## Engineering Excellence

OpenPS3 values clean architecture over shortcuts.

Documentation over assumptions.

Verification over speculation.

Reproducibility over hacks.

Every subsystem should be understandable.

Every interface should be documented.

Every design decision should have a rationale.

---

## Community

OpenPS3 is built by contributors.

Developers.

Researchers.

Reverse engineers.

Designers.

Writers.

Testers.

Everyone has a place.

Ideas compete on technical merit.

Contributors are respected regardless of experience.

---

## Modularity

No monolithic architecture.

Every subsystem should evolve independently.

Kernel.

Drivers.

SDK.

Graphics.

Networking.

Package management.

User interface.

Documentation.

Everything should be replaceable.

---

## Longevity

OpenPS3 is designed to survive decades.

Every decision should maximize maintainability.

Short-term convenience must never compromise long-term sustainability.

---

# Project Goals

OpenPS3 aims to build:

* Complete public documentation of the PS3 architecture.
* A modern open SDK.
* A modular operating environment.
* Native development tools.
* Community package repositories.
* A modern graphical shell inspired by, but independent from, the original user experience.
* A portable toolchain.
* Long-term preservation infrastructure.

---

# Technical Philosophy

OpenPS3 prefers documented interfaces over undocumented behavior.

We prefer architecture over patches.

Design over improvisation.

Stable APIs over hidden dependencies.

Public specifications over tribal knowledge.

The objective is not merely to make things work.

The objective is to make them understandable.

---

# Open Knowledge

Documentation is a first-class deliverable.

Every discovery should be recorded.

Every subsystem should have specifications.

Every interface should have examples.

Every contributor should be able to understand how the platform works.

Knowledge should never depend on a single individual.

---

# Preservation Before Replacement

Whenever possible, OpenPS3 seeks to preserve compatibility while building independent alternatives.

Existing software should continue to have a path forward.

New software should never depend exclusively on proprietary infrastructure.

---

# The Future

We envision a future where a PlayStation 3 purchased twenty years ago can still:

* boot instantly,
* receive community software,
* run modern open applications,
* serve as a media center,
* act as a development machine,
* host services,
* teach computer architecture,
* inspire new generations of engineers.

Not because a corporation continues to support it.

But because its community does.
