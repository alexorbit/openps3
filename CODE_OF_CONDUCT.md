# OpenPS3 Code of Conduct

## Our Purpose

OpenPS3 exists to preserve knowledge, advance engineering, and build an open platform for the PlayStation 3.

This project brings together people from different backgrounds, countries, disciplines, and levels of experience.

We believe excellent engineering is only possible in an environment built on professionalism, respect, curiosity, and intellectual honesty.

This Code of Conduct exists to protect that environment.

---

# Our Values

Every interaction within the OpenPS3 community should reflect the following values.

## Respect

Treat every contributor with respect.

Respect does not require agreement.

Technical disagreement is expected.

Personal disrespect is not.

---

## Professionalism

Focus discussions on engineering.

Keep conversations constructive.

Critique ideas.

Do not attack people.

---

## Intellectual Honesty

Admit uncertainty.

Differentiate facts from assumptions.

Document unknowns.

Changing your opinion after new evidence is a strength, not a weakness.

---

## Curiosity

Questions are encouraged.

Teaching is encouraged.

Learning is encouraged.

Every contributor was once a beginner.

---

## Collaboration

Knowledge should be shared.

Documentation should be written.

Experiments should be reproducible.

Success belongs to the project, not to individuals.

---

# Expected Behavior

Members of the OpenPS3 community are expected to:

* communicate respectfully;
* assume good faith;
* support claims with evidence;
* explain technical decisions;
* welcome constructive feedback;
* document discoveries;
* help new contributors understand the project;
* prioritize long-term project health over personal preference.

---

# Technical Discussions

Engineering discussions should be guided by evidence.

Good arguments include:

* measurements
* benchmarks
* architecture
* documentation
* specifications
* reproducible experiments
* standards
* implementation experience

Poor arguments include:

* "because I prefer it"
* "because another project does it"
* "because it has always been done this way"

Evidence should guide decisions.

---

# Disagreement

Disagreement is normal.

Many important engineering decisions emerge from healthy debate.

When disagreements occur:

* remain respectful;
* focus on the technical problem;
* listen carefully;
* consider alternatives;
* seek consensus whenever possible.

If consensus cannot be reached, the project's governance process applies.

---

# Inclusive Community

OpenPS3 welcomes contributors regardless of:

* nationality
* language
* age
* profession
* education
* experience level

The quality of a contribution is determined by its technical merit, not by the identity of its author.

---

# Mentorship

Experienced contributors are encouraged to mentor newer contributors.

Examples include:

* reviewing pull requests;
* explaining architecture;
* improving documentation;
* answering questions;
* suggesting learning resources.

Strong communities create future maintainers.

---

# Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable:

* harassment;
* personal attacks;
* discrimination;
* intimidation;
* deliberate misinformation;
* abusive language;
* trolling;
* doxxing;
* threats;
* repeated disruption of technical discussions;
* knowingly submitting malicious code.

These behaviors undermine the project's goals and may result in moderation.

---

# Engineering Integrity

OpenPS3 places a high value on engineering integrity.

Contributors should:

* accurately report experimental results;
* acknowledge limitations;
* cite sources when appropriate;
* avoid presenting speculation as fact;
* correct mistakes when discovered.

Engineering depends on trust.

---

# Documentation Culture

Documentation is considered a first-class contribution.

Writing clear documentation is as valuable as writing excellent code.

A documented discovery benefits every future contributor.

---

# Code Review

Reviews should focus on:

* correctness;
* architecture;
* maintainability;
* security;
* documentation;
* testing.

Review comments should explain *why* improvements are suggested.

Constructive reviews improve both code and contributors.

---

# Leadership

Project maintainers are expected to:

* act consistently;
* communicate transparently;
* explain decisions;
* encourage participation;
* resolve conflicts fairly;
* prioritize the long-term health of the project.

Leadership exists to serve the project and its community.

---

# Reporting Concerns

If you experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it privately using the project's designated reporting process.

Reports will be handled confidentially whenever practical.

Retaliation against reporters is not acceptable.

---

# Enforcement

Responses to violations may include:

* private guidance;
* formal warning;
* temporary restriction;
* removal of specific privileges;
* suspension;
* permanent removal from the project.

The response should be proportional to the severity and frequency of the behavior.

---

# Decision Making

Moderation decisions should:

* be documented internally;
* follow established governance processes;
* remain consistent;
* prioritize community safety and project integrity.

Whenever appropriate, decisions should include a clear explanation.

---

# Our Engineering Culture

OpenPS3 values:

* evidence over opinion;
* architecture over improvisation;
* documentation over assumptions;
* collaboration over competition;
* long-term maintainability over short-term convenience.

These principles guide both our software and our community.

---

# Final Statement

OpenPS3 is more than a software project.

It is a long-term effort to preserve knowledge, document a remarkable computing platform, and create an open ecosystem for future generations.

The quality of our community will determine the quality of our engineering.

Let us build both with the same care.
